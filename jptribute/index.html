JOHN PEEL

Here's John Peel's Bio:

John Peel was born the day before the outbreak of the Second World War in 1939. His 38-year career as a radio DJ is the stuff of legend and the bands he went on to discover too numerous to mention, though David Bowie, Roxy Music, T Rex, Genesis, Joy Division, New Order, The Smiths, Radiohead and the White Stripes would do as a start. He lived in Suffolk with his wife Sheila and their children William, Thomas, Alexandra and Florence, plus various dogs and cats, until his death in October 2004.

Here's a timeline of John Peel's life:

  • 1939 - Born John Robert Parker Ravenscroft in Heswall, Cheshire, England

  • 1957 - Starts two-year National Service in the Royal Artillery as a B2 radar operator

  • 1960 - Moves to Texas, U.S to work at a cotton mill

  • 1963 - Got first radio job, unpaid, at WRR(AM) in Dallas

  • 1965 - Works presenting show on KMEN in San Bernardino, California, using the name John Ravencroft

  • 1967 - Returns to England. Works with the offshore pirate radio station Radio London under the name John Peel. His programme is eventually named The Perfumed Garden

  • 1967 - Radio London closes down and John joins the BBC's new pop music station, BBC Radio 1. He would stay there until his death in 2004

  • 1968 - First time presenting Top Of The Pops. Would appear 49 times on show between 1968-1995

  • 1974 - Marries Sheila Gilhooly. They go on to have four children and remain together for rest of his life

  • 1976 - The punk era begins and John devotes large parts of his show to punk bands like The Ramones

  • 1978 - First plays "Teenage Kicks" by The Undertones on his show. Is famously his all-time favourite song. His headstone would be engraved with it's lyrics "Teenage dreams, so hard to beat"

  • 1998 - Began hosting "Home Truths", a documentary program on BBC Radio 4

  • 1998 - Appointed an OBE for services to British music

  • 2004 - Dies at the age of 65
